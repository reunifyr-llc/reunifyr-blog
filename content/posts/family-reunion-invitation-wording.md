The invitation sets the tone for the whole reunion. Get it right and people RSVP fast — get it wrong and you're chasing relatives for weeks. Below are 25 ready-to-use invitation templates for every reunion style, plus the must-include details that separate good invites from ignored ones.

## What Every Family Reunion Invitation Must Include

Before the templates, the non-negotiable details:

1. **Family name** (e.g., "The Johnson Family Reunion")
2. **Date and start/end times**
3. **Full venue address** — including the pavilion or building number
4. **Host names** and how to contact them
5. **RSVP deadline** with phone, email, or website link
6. **What to bring** — covered dish, lawn chair, swim suit, gift for elders
7. **Cost** — even if it's $0, say it

Skip any of these and the questions will eat your week.

## Final Tips

- **Send invitations at least 6 weeks ahead.** For destination reunions, 4–6 months.
- **Always include an RSVP deadline.** Without one, you'll get half your responses the day before.
- **Repeat the date and address** at the bottom of every invite — people scan, they don't read.
- **Use a family website** if you can — it stops the "what time again?" texts cold.
